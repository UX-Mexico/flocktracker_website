Flocktracker

The Flocktracker Android-based smartphone application advances traditional data collecting methodology, enabling the creation of robust data sets quickly and easily.

State of the Application

Flocktracker has evolved substantially from its original Mexico City build, back in May of 2013. From user interface to overall functionality, the application has completely rebuilt the original design. The new system is far more stable, accurate, and versatile than it has ever been. Organization Overview

Flocktracker is a tool developed as a robust and flexible research tool for ongoing studies in both Dhaka, Bangladesh and Mexico City, Mexico. The project is the result of an ongoing effort under the research of MIT professor P. Christopher Zegras’ Mobility Futures Collaborative in the Department of Urban Studies and Planning. The current application is based off of initial designs produced with Urban Launchpad in the mapping of Dhaka’s bus system to create the city’s first complete bus map. More can be found on this effort by searching “Urban Launchpad" in either Facebook or Google.
